Introduction Service
CDISC
The Clinical Data Interchange Standards Consortium (CDISC) is an open, non-profit organization that develops and supports global data standards to improve the quality and interoperability of medical research and healthcare.
EMA
The European Medicines Agency (EMA) protects and promotes human and animal health by evaluating and monitoring medicines within the European Union (EU) and the European Economic Area (EEA)
FDA
The Food and Drug Administration (FDA) is responsible for protecting the public health by assuring the safety, efficacy, and security of human and veterinary drugs, biological products, medical devices, our nation's food supply, cosmetics, and products that emit radiation.
Search Portal
Search Portal brings the user to the three customized searches mentioned in the introduction.
Welcome to Angela's Noether's Search Portal, a site especially designed to search for research on clinical trials being conducted in Taiwan or Taiwan researchers cooperate with. These searches are all directed at the US National Health Institute's (NIH) database. The NIH database is one of the largest databases covering clinical trials conducted around the world, including Taiwan. Software searches for clinical trials usually require the user to insert a therapeutic area. A comprehensive list of therapeutic areas may be found by browsing the following sites:
  • The Clinical Data Interchange Standards Consortium (CDISC) webpage
  • The European Medicines Agency (EMA) website
  • The Food and Drug Administration (FDA) web site
For people living in Taiwan or researching Taiwan Clinical Trial studies, it is important to have dedicated search filter that brings back all studies being done on Taiwan. Also, I have built additional searches on my web page The "Search Portal" button brings up a web page that is completely dedicated to exclusively searching studies going on in Taiwan. On the left hand side please click on the "Service" button to continue to the next page: This page introduces four buttons: the CDSIC button introduces therapeutic areas listed on the CDSIC web site, EMA button introduces the therapeutic areas listed on the EMA web site, FDA button introduces therapeutic areas listed on the FDA site. Search Portal button brings the user to three customized searches, the Search research button allows the user to search the NIH database site by therapeutic area. The second button allows the user to search by name of the researcher; it is sorted by the number of clinical trials the researcher(s) have performed. The Search by Clinical Trial Phase button allows the user to search by phase or phases of clinical trials in the selected therapeutic area.
